Core AI & Machine Learning Fundamentals

The building blocks of artificial intelligence and machine learning

Concepts 1-15: From basic AI to advanced learning methods

Fundamental Concept #1
Artificial Intelligence (AI)
The field of computer science focused on creating systems capable of performing tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, perception, and decision-making.
A robot vacuum using sensors to avoid obstacles and clean your room.
Learning Method #2
Machine Learning (ML)
A subset of AI where systems learn from data to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed for every scenario.
A spam filter learning from email examples to detect spam.
Learning Type #3
Supervised Learning
A type of ML where the model learns from labeled training data to make predictions.
Predicting house prices from historical data with known prices.
Learning Type #4
Unsupervised Learning
A type of ML where the model identifies patterns and relationships in unlabeled data.
Grouping similar customers in a marketing dataset without labels.
Learning Type #5
Reinforcement Learning
An ML method where agents learn to make decisions by receiving rewards or penalties.
A game AI learning to win by trial and error, like in chess or Go.
Core Architecture #6
Neural Networks
Computing systems inspired by biological neural networks, consisting of interconnected nodes (neurons) that process information.
Image recognition systems using layers of artificial neurons to identify objects.
Advanced Method #7
Deep Learning
A subset of ML using neural networks with many layers to learn complex patterns in data.
Face recognition in photos using multiple layers of feature detection.
Data Type #8
Training Data
The dataset used to teach a machine learning model, containing input-output examples.
Thousands of labeled cat and dog photos used to train an image classifier.
Data Type #9
Testing Data
A separate dataset used to evaluate a model's performance after training.
New, unseen images used to test how well the trained model recognizes cats and dogs.
Model Problem #10
Overfitting
When a model learns the training data too specifically and fails to generalize to new data.
A model that memorizes exam answers but can't solve new, similar problems.
Model Problem #11
Underfitting
When a model is too simple to capture the underlying patterns in the data.
Using a straight line to predict data that actually follows a curve.
Core Component #12
Model
A mathematical representation of a real-world process, trained to make predictions or decisions.
A weather prediction model that forecasts rain based on temperature and humidity.
Core Component #13
Algorithm
A set of rules or instructions that a computer follows to solve problems or perform tasks.
A step-by-step recipe for sorting a list of numbers from smallest to largest.
Ethical Issue #14
Bias in AI
Unfair preferences or discrimination in AI systems, often reflecting biases in training data or design.
A hiring algorithm that unfairly favors certain demographics due to biased historical data.
Ethical Framework #15
Ethics in AI
The study of moral principles and guidelines for developing and deploying AI systems responsibly.
Ensuring facial recognition systems respect privacy and don't discriminate.

Generative AI & Creative Technologies

AI systems that create new content and handle creative tasks

Concepts 16-25: From content generation to advanced techniques

Creative AI #16
Generative AI (GenAI)
AI systems that create new content (text, images, audio, video) based on patterns learned from training data.
ChatGPT writing stories or DALL-E creating artwork from text descriptions.
User Input #17
Prompt
Instructions or input text given to an AI system to guide its output generation.
"Write a poem about autumn leaves" as input to a text generation model.
Optimization Technique #18
Prompt Engineering
The practice of designing effective prompts to get better results from AI systems.
Adding "step-by-step" to a math prompt to get detailed solutions instead of just answers.
AI Capability #19
Creativity in AI
The ability of AI systems to generate novel, valuable, and meaningful content or solutions.
AI composing original music or designing unique architectural concepts.
Generation Method #20
Diffusion Models
AI models that generate images by gradually removing noise from random data.
Stable Diffusion creating detailed artwork by iteratively refining noisy images.
Generation Method #21
GANs (Generative Adversarial Networks)
Two neural networks competing against each other to generate realistic data.
Creating realistic human faces that don't exist or generating fake but convincing videos.
Generation Process #22
Sampling
The process of generating outputs from a trained model using probability distributions.
An AI choosing the next word in a sentence based on learned probabilities.
Training Method #23
Fine-tuning
Adapting a pre-trained model for a specific task by training it on domain-specific data.
Customizing a general language model to write medical reports by training on medical texts.
Learning Approach #24
Zero-shot Learning
A model's ability to perform tasks it wasn't explicitly trained for.
A language model translating between languages it never specifically learned.
Learning Approach #25
Few-shot Learning
Training a model to perform new tasks with only a few examples.
Teaching an AI to write poetry by showing it just 3-5 poem examples.

Natural Language Processing (NLP)

How AI understands, processes, and generates human language

Concepts 26-35: From text processing to speech technologies

NLP Foundation #26
Natural Language Processing (NLP)
A field of AI focused on enabling computers to understand, interpret, and generate human language.
Google Translate converting text between languages or Siri understanding spoken commands.
Text Processing #27
Tokenization
Breaking down text into smaller units (tokens) like words, subwords, or characters for processing.
Splitting "Hello world!" into tokens: ["Hello", "world", "!"].
Text Analysis #28
Part-of-Speech Tagging
Identifying grammatical roles of words in sentences (noun, verb, adjective, etc.).
Tagging "The quick brown fox jumps" as article, adjective, adjective, noun, verb.
Information Extraction #29
Named Entity Recognition (NER)
Identifying and classifying named entities in text (people, places, organizations, dates).
Recognizing "Apple" as a company and "Steve Jobs" as a person in news articles.
Text Analysis #30
Sentiment Analysis
Determining the emotional tone or opinion expressed in text (positive, negative, neutral).
Analyzing customer reviews to determine if they're satisfied or disappointed with a product.
Text Processing #31
Text Classification
Automatically categorizing text documents into predefined classes or topics.
Sorting emails into folders like "Work," "Personal," or "Promotions."
Language Technology #32
Machine Translation
Automatically translating text or speech from one language to another using AI.
Google Translate converting "Hello" from English to "Hola" in Spanish.
Content Processing #33
Text Summarization
Automatically creating shorter versions of longer texts while preserving key information.
Creating a 2-paragraph summary of a 10-page research paper.
Speech Technology #34
Speech Recognition
Converting spoken language into text using AI algorithms.
Voice assistants like Siri transcribing "What's the weather?" into text.
Speech Technology #35
Text-to-Speech (TTS)
Converting written text into spoken audio using artificial voices.
GPS navigation systems reading driving directions aloud from text instructions.

Large Language Models & Advanced NLP

The sophisticated architectures powering modern language AI

Concepts 36-45: From transformers to reasoning capabilities

Advanced Model #36
Large Language Model (LLM)
Massive neural networks trained on enormous amounts of text to understand and generate human-like language.
GPT-4 or Claude answering questions and having conversations across many topics.
Model Architecture #37
Transformer Architecture
A neural network design that revolutionized NLP by using attention mechanisms to process sequences efficiently.
The foundation of models like ChatGPT, enabling them to understand context across long conversations.
Core Mechanism #38
Attention Mechanism
A technique that helps models focus on relevant parts of input when making predictions.
When translating "The cat sat on the mat," paying more attention to "cat" when generating "gato" in Spanish.
Training Phase #39
Pre-training
The initial training phase where models learn language patterns from massive text datasets.
Training GPT on billions of web pages, books, and articles to learn general language understanding.
Model Limitation #40
Context Window
The maximum amount of text (in tokens) that a model can consider at once.
A model with a 4,000-token limit forgetting the beginning of a very long conversation.
Text Unit #41
Token
Basic units of text that language models process, often words or parts of words.
"Hello world" might be split into tokens: ["Hello", " world"] for processing.
Model Operation #42
Inference
The process of using a trained model to generate outputs or make predictions on new data.
Running ChatGPT to answer your question after it has already been trained.
Model Issue #43
Hallucination (in LLMs)
When language models generate false or fabricated information that sounds plausible but isn't true.
An AI confidently stating a fake historical fact or citing non-existent research papers.
Reasoning Method #44
Chain-of-Thought Reasoning
A technique where models break down complex problems into step-by-step reasoning processes.
Solving "If John has 5 apples and gives away 2, how many are left?" by showing: "5 - 2 = 3 apples remaining."
Advanced AI #45
Multimodal AI
AI systems that can process and generate multiple types of data (text, images, audio, video).
GPT-4 Vision analyzing photos and describing what it sees, or DALL-E creating images from text.
